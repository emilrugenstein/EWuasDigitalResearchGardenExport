Workshop (Collective Action and Nature Protection)

  • Topic: How to act together to protect nature?
  • Method: Develop, brainstorm or/and play games which revolve around that question.
    • Caroline Archambault proposed a plan A, B, and C strategy
      • plan A: "develop games together"
        • inspired by the Game of Games approach, form small groups which have to creatively develop their own games around the given topic and with possibly some given constraints on "mandatory" game mechanics, game utensils, and setting
      • plan B: "hacking games they already know"
        • take games they already now and think about how you could convert them into something where people learn about X
      • plan C: "what do you think about these ideas and games?"
        • ask them what they already know about biodiversity loss /collective action and try game which we developed before

Structure (60-90min)

Intro (5min)

  • Introduce myself and ask some general questions about the class to warm up
  • Split class into groups of around 4 and hand out paper
    • possibly consult a teacher for good group splits

Group Topic Exploration (10min)

  • Give the groups time to write down answers on the following questions
  • On Collective Action:
    • Where do people act together?
    • How can people act together?
    • When do people fail to act together?
  • On Nature Protection
    • Why is a healthy nature important to humans? (Potentially only verbally together to save time)
    • What problems does our nature have right now? (Potentially only verbally as well)
    • How can we protect nature?

Answer Sharing (5min)

  • Collect some answers, but this should not be exhaustive
    • good way to do this is in a lightning format -> every group says after each other one answer that has not been said before

Group Game Exploration (5-10min)

  • Collect games they know and like and make them think about them
  • Ask to answer the following questions:
    • What games do you like? Why?
      • But ask them to go beyond big ball sports i.e. football, volleyball, netball and handball
    • What do players learn in these games?

Answer Sharing and Game Design Prompt (5-10min)

  • Again collect some answers
  • Now, ask them to design a game in which the players learn something about how we can protect nature by acting together by either
    • a) coming up with new games
    • or b) modifying the collected games
  • Provide some inspiration by
    • providing list with materials, which they can but don't have to use:
      • small stones
      • sand
      • timer/clock
      • cups
      • dice
      • ball
      • pen and paper
      • string
      • anything else they can think of
    • prompting them that students like them should like to play it
    • reminding them to think back to the answers of the previous two sections

Option A&B: Group Game Design (20-30min)

  • Go around and help brainstorming
  • It's ok and expectable that they will be probably be a bit confused for the first 5 - 10 mins
  • But if all goes good, then here the magic happens
    • If not jump to Option C

Option C: Introduce and play own game (20-25min)

  • Explain game and play it first with one group while others are watching (10min)
    • NG1 Maasai Village
  • Then let them play together in groups (10-15min)
    • possibly merge groups so the groups are better oversee-able
    • encourage them to play multiple times

Share Results (5-10min)

  • Option A&B:
    • Share different games and let the groups explain it
    • Encourage a concise explanation as this otherwise can easily crash the time frame
  • Option C:
    • Ask them to reflect on the game play and what different strategies where used and to which results they led
  • Close
