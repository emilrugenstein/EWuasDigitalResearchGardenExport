Other games

  • With certain clapping patterns:
    • Police Central: Children in circle and "pass on" a clap by receiving on the right and passing on with the left hand. At the end of a singing song that kid can shoot one of the other kids from the circle. Than this is repeated again without that kid.
      • Played at Emirishoi Primary School when visited by Ezekiel Roimen and Emil Rugenstein on 17-07-2025.
      • Video: Game-PoliceCentral.mp4
    • Sumsum: Another clapping game whereby 4 kids, standing in a rectangle, clap in a certain pattern with both hands. Sometimes across each other, sometimes changing to the left or right partner. The song continues into the ABC and the game is started again when one of the kids misses the correct pattern.
      • Played at Emirishoi Primary School when visited by Ezekiel Roimen and Emil Rugenstein on 17-07-2025
      • Video: Game-Sumsum.mp4
  • Colour jumping game: Kids play outside and draw three or more long fields into the sand in which they line up. Every line has one colour and one kid now screams one colour after another and the other kids have to jump to that line/column. The catch is that the kid can scream non-existing colours . If kids move at a call of a colour that is not included or if they jump to the wrong column they have to step to the side. The game continues till only one kid is left.
    • Witnessed by Emil Rugenstein while walking around in Elangata Wuas Town
    • Video: Game-ColourJumping.mp4

Games on Collective Action and Nature Protection

The goal is to develop new games which make people think about how people can protect nature by acting together. The process itself of designing such games can be a way to spark thoughts and ideas on this topic. Playing and designing these games can also be used to observe how people think and act collectively.

Background Knowledge about the Maasai

Maasai age groups

There are five traditional stages in the life of a Maasai

  1. Young ones
    • Enkipaata -> circumcision as transition to next group (newly circumcised)
      • traditionally with around 20 years, but nowadays often done earlier
  2. Newly circumcised
    • Shaving ceremony as transition to next group
      • nowadays boys can decide if the go back to school afterwards or the become a real Morang
  3. Warriors (Morran)
    • Climax of Maasai live
    • The often grow their hair very long
    • Another very big ceremony (called "Eunoto") which ends the Morran-Ship
  4. Young elders
    • Last ceremony is the "Olngesher" where the younger left hand group and the right hand group are joined together as one cohort
  5. Elders
